Definition of Cybercrime

  • Cybercrime refers to criminal activities carried out via computers or networks, targeting people, companies, or countries.
  • It includes activities like hacking, identity theft, cyberbullying, phishing, and online fraud.
  • Laws such as the Computer Fraud and Abuse Act regulate cyber offenses.

Types of Cybercrime

  • Phishing: Impersonating entities to steal information
  • Unauthorized access & hacking: Breaking into secured systems
  • Ransomware: Encrypting files for ransom payments
  • Identity Theft: Using stolen data for criminal gains
  • Cyberbullying & harassment: Online intimidation
  • Botnets & DDoS: Disrupting networks/services
  • Spyware: Illegally monitoring and stealing data

Consequences of Cybercrime

  • Financial losses
  • Brand damage & loss of customer trust
  • Legal penalties
  • Psychological trauma
  • National security risks

Prevention and Protection

  • Strong passwords & two-factor authentication
  • Regular data backups
  • User education & awareness
  • Incident response plans
  • Advanced cybersecurity software

Future of Cybercrime

  • AI-powered attacks
  • Stricter global regulations
  • Innovation in cybersecurity tools
  • International cooperation for prevention
  • Growing need for public awareness
